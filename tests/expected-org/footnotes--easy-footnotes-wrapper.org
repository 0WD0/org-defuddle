* The History of Ancient Logistics

:WORD_COUNT: 166

This is the first part of a series examining ancient supply chains. Although the kingdom has an unfair reputation for being a land of peaceful traders, [fn:1] it was in fact a formidable military power — eclipsed only by its greatest rival.

The kingdom is notable for being the only power that seriously challenged its rival on even footing, pushing them to the brink of defeat /twice/. [fn:2]

Peak mobilization reached approximately 165,000 soldiers under arms. [fn:3] We will work through the components of that force and note how each changed over time.

[fn:1] A. Smith, /A History of Naval Power Before the Great War/ (1954), 334. To say this reputation contradicts our sources would be a /*staggering*/ understatement.

[fn:2] Calculating the combined losses is necessarily guesswork, but the heaviest losses were approximately 2,200, with the rest below 1,000 each.

[fn:3] That is /double/ the peak mobilization of any other ancient state. For more on maximum mobilization figures, see M.J. Taylor, /[[https://example.com/soldiers-and-silver][Soldiers & Silver]]/ (2020).
