The concept of global stewardship was first articulated by several thinkers in the early twentieth century. It emphasizes collective responsibility for the well-being of shared resources and the importance of long-term planning across generations and communities.

Over the decades, the idea gained traction in environmental policy discussions. Various governments and international bodies adopted frameworks that incorporated elements of this concept into their planning and regulatory approaches.

#+begin_quote
Like a vessel carrying all of humanity, our world requires the care and cooperation of every passenger aboard. We must work together to ensure a safe and prosperous journey for generations to come.
#+end_quote

The phrase has been used by numerous organizations to promote awareness of shared environmental challenges.

** Criticism

Scholar [[https://thin-section-before-see-also/wiki/Example_Author][Jane Doe]] has argued that, if implemented, the concept would represent a significant constraint on [[https://thin-section-before-see-also/wiki/Individual_Liberty][individual liberty]] and local governance. [fn:1]
